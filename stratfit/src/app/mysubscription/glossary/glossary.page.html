About Program Structure
  1. A repetition, also known as a rep for short, is one completion of an exercise: one Deadlift, one Bench press, one Squat.
  2. A set is a series of repetitions. For example, eight repetitions can be one set of bench presses.
  3. Microcycle: A micro-cycle is defined as a number of training sessions, built around a given combination of acute program variables, which include progression as well as alternating effort (heavy vs. light days).
    1. In Starter program, the exercise progresses rapidly â€“ Progression is planned for every successive session with a break day in-between.
    2. In Intermediate program, the micro cycle is one week long and progression can happen at the end of Microcycle, if the progression rule is designed to trigger at Microcycle.
  4. Mesocycle: A Mesocycle can be defined as a number of continuous weeks where the training program emphasizes the same type of physical adaptations.
    1. There are no Mesocycle in starter program
    2. In an Intermediate / Advanced program and progression can happen at the end of Mesocycle, if the progression rule is designed to trigger at Mesocycle.
  5. Period: A unit of training time which consists of Mesocycles which are sequenced to bring about a certain overall training effect.
    1. General Period: The period of the Macrocycle in which General Physical Preparedness (as opposed to Special Physical Preparedness) is primarily focused on and developed. The primary goal of the general period is to prepare the athlete's physical system for the specialized work to come in the Specific Period. The GP follows the end of a competitive season, and at it's beginning has the goal of ensuring full restoration of the athlete's physical system before more accumulative training is done.
    2. Specific Period: The period of the macrocycle which has the purpose of transforming the General Physical Preparedness developed in the General Period into Special Physical Preparedness required in competition or in the competitive season, or to reach a specific goal of a non-competitive trainee. It occurs concurrently with the time that technical and tactical coaches call "pre-season" since it leads directly into the in-season competitive period. At this point the sport coaches will be honing athlete's technical and tactical skills, while the strength training is specializing his/her physical fitness to best utilize the skill in the sport.
    3. In-Season Period: The period of the macrocycle in which the main competions occur. Training in the this period is designed to maintain the special physical preparedness attained during the specific period while ensuring the athletes physical readiness is kept optimal for competions/matches/games.
  6. Break day: Rest Period between session days
  7. Off day: Weekly day off (7th day from Start of the Program)
